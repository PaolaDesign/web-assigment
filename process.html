Our process looks like this:

All learning solutions the Future Investments learning team creates are grounded in the Tā Tātou Rautaki Akoranga | Our Waka Kotahi learning strategy. To help us design the best solutions to address learning needs we apply a human centred design thinking approach.

Design thinking allows us to design with you. By understanding both yours and your end users desired outcomes, challenges, and assumptions we can accurately define the problems we are trying to solve. By taking a non-linear, iterative process we can innovate, prototype and test to ensure robust, fit for purpose learning solutions. By checking in early and often we save time and create solutions that work for our customers.

1. Trigger

The product owner/problem owner needs to connect with the Future Investments learning manager to request support.

2. Prioritisation

At their monthly meeting the Investment Leads rōpū will consider all requests for learning support and make decisions on the prioritisation of this work.

Once this decision has been made a learning consultant to begin work with you.

3. Performance consultation

A learning consultant will meet with the product owner/problem owner to conduct a performance consultation.

Expectations from the product owner/problem owner:

You’ll need to provide us with some subject matter experts to work with who can provide their technical expertise to development of learning.

4. SME consultation

This is an opportunity to meet with the subject matter experts to further scope and understand the learning required and how best to deliver this.

Expectations from the SME:

We will need you to provide an technical guidelines you have available on the change/event as well as providing valuable insights during the consultation.

5. Concept brief

We will design a prototype based on our performance consulting and SME consultation. The concept brief outlines what learning could look like and how to deliver it.

Expectations of product owner/problem owner:

Review final draft and sign off unless you have delegated authority to the SME.

6. Content drafts

We may ask you to review draft content, which could be new or a review of existing content. We will provide guidance on what you need to check for existing content.

Expectations from the SME:

Follow best practice guidelines for review. Review within two drafts within the time given.

7. Product build

Once you have signed off the content draft, we will build learning in the chosen platform.

Expectations from the product owner/problem owner:

Review final draft and sign off unless you have delegated authority to the subject matter expert.

8. Final product

Once you sign off the product build draft, we will create a final version.

Expectations from the product owner/problem owner:

Review final product and final sign off for Go Live.

9. Post-launch

Once learning reaches a specific ‘shelf life’ we will review the learning, its impact.

Expectations from the product owner/problem owner:

We may need to restart the design process or ask you to review existing content.
